Who I'm Following

Micro-Influencers

27 July 2018, Friday

Some may say a micro-influencer is someone with less than 100,000 Instagram followers. Others might think 10,000 is the cutoff. Whatever the true limit is, I’m sticking with those who have less than a 10,000 following for this post. These guys are just as important as any macro-influencer around. Even though their audience might be on a smaller scale, their viewers seem to be a lot more genuine and dedicated to their exposure. Another thing I like about these guys is how relatable they are. Micro-influencers are just normal every day people with a hobby. I decided to narrow down 3 of my favorite menswear micro-influencers I follow:

1. Joachim Aamodt (@joachimaamodt)

If you follow a lot of menswear bloggers, flat lays often get a little redundant and just aren’t very creative. Joachim is an exception. He does flat lays in a way that is inspiring and actually makes you enjoy the fit. It may seem like such an easy thing to do but Joachim adds the perfect touch to each of his photos for some authentic and original content. Having just started a little over a year ago, this guy’s got heavy potential for his future in flat lays. For those who don’t have a ton of knowledge in men’s fashion and would like to know more, Joachim is a great place to start.

2. Anthony Lee (@anthonyylee_)

Anthony has one of the most authentic fits around right now. It’s easy for people to get sucked into the minimal Scandinavian streetwear trend, but Anthony has got his own look. He takes workwear and gives it a fresh, new look with neutrals and earth tones. If you’re one for straight-legged cropped trousers and dad hats, give this guy a follow.

3. David Joseph (@_david_joseph)

David is king when it comes to creative content. He just recently started doing Photoshop tutorials for posts he’s done in the past to show you how they were put together. Unlike most who resort to a white or black color flow, his grid consists of mainly navy hues which I can’t get enough of. If I had to give you one reason to follow David it would be for the use of shadows in every one of his photos. You can tell a lot of thought goes into each angle which makes him all the more worth the follow.
