Fashion Tip

Style On a Budget

17 August 2018, Friday

I get it. Money’s tight but you can’t tell what’s hurting worse - your wallet or your wardrobe. Luckily there are plenty of companies out there with the goal to create high quality products at a fraction of the price. Below are some essentials to help grow your closet without blowing your bank account.

1. Uniqlo: Supima Cotton Crewneck T-Shirt ($10)

This brand likes to keep it basic. They are my go-to for all my t-shirts not only for the price, but the quality. Their t-shirts are made from Supima cotton which allows for an extra soft feel throughout the day. Usually price always determines the quality you’ll get but Uniqlo proves this theory wrong.

2. A Day's March: Slim Fit Trouser ($95)

This brand’s purpose is to create high quality essentials at a fair price, so it’s no surprise that I would mention them as a brand to purchase from on a budget. Trousers are something you don’t want to mess with because with them tailoring is everything. A Day’s March offers pristine tailoring to their trousers for a perfectly slim fit. If you are like me and your legs run a little slimmer than average, find a tailor you trust to taper or hem them. It’s important to make sure your trousers fit just right in order to keep the shape they were designed for.

3. Good Guys: Samo White Sneaker ($113)

Now I know these aren’t exactly a cheap option for someone on a budget, but I think it’s important to make sure you’ve got a quality pair of shoes. It might seem like a huge investment, but over time you’ll see how much longer they last than the $40 sneakers you bought from H&M. The key to purchasing a sneaker that lasts is to make sure the soles are sewn rather than glued. This will keep the soles intact for much longer and help keep the shape of the shoe. What makes these a great go-to sneaker is that they go with everything (plus they are great for the environment). Whether you’re walking around town or on a night out with friends, these are the perfect addition to your fit.

4. EyeBuyDirect: Sun Kyoto (Non-Prescription: $42, Prescription: $62)

If you wear glasses, you know how incredibly expensive it can be to purchase a pair of sunglasses with a prescription. EyeBuyDirect solves this problem by offering quality sunglasses, prescription and non-prescription, for a fraction of the price. If you catch them at the right time, they also often have a buy one get one promotion going on.
