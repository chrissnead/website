Who I'm Following: Micro-Influencers Pt. 2

22 August 2018, Friday

A couple weeks ago I recommended some micro-influencers who I feel are slowly gaining recognition in the menswear community. I want to do that today but with those above a 10k following. I’ll say it again, the thing I love most about micro-influencers is that they are just ordinary people with a passion for anything from fashion to travel, and sometimes both. I find myself keeping up with their content a lot more than the guys with 100k+ followers and I think the reason behind this is because they’re relatable. I’ve communicated with many of these guys, sometimes with a general question and other times just to chat about our similar likings on topics of fashion. If you’re trying to get into the menswear community or want some quality advice to step up your fashion game, check these guys out:

1. Harry Spain (@hryspn)

If you’re needing a good grid inspiration, Harry is your guy. His wardrobe seems to consist of many muted basics and autumn tones. I find a lot of inspiration from his page just because it’s so aesthetically pleasing. I don’t know why but his photos often remind me of a cold rainy day on the coast. Although that may not be his intention, I always know a mr.fits post when I see one.

2. Harry Young (@harryhas)

I’ve been keeping up with Harry Young for quite some time. He’s one influencer I feel shares a lot of the same fashion tastes as me, so I often find myself scrolling through his content more than others. He has recently been experimenting with some really creative shots that take fashion and lifestyle blogging to a whole new level. I love that he is constantly improving and keeping it interesting because it’s so easy to stick to the same type of content.

3. Jordan Bunker (@jordanbunker)

If you’ve got any questions regarding menswear this guy is the one to talk to. He’s got a lot of knowledge and connections in the fashion industry and can also provide you with some helpful advice on anything from travel to influencer marketing. He’s got a nice blog with some interesting reads so if you enjoy those subscribe to his newsletters. Jordan has been a lot of my inspiration for creating a unique brand for myself and writing blogs on topics I’m passionate about. His shadow game isn’t too bad either.
