A Simple Recipe with the Always Pan



Just a quick recipe for those cold winter evenings.




While a lot of the world is getting back into their normal routines, I’m currently keeping quarantined as Portland is still closed for the most part. With all the time I’ve got on my hands, I’ve taken the past few months to understand my skin more to keep my face looking healthy and young with minimal breakouts. I tend to lean more towards the oily side, so my skin is much more prone to breakouts than most. Though it has lessened over time, this is something I’ve struggled with since my teen years and never really learned how to manage it correctly. If you had asked me a year ago, I’d tell you my skincare routine was a basic two-step face wash and daily moisturizer. This isn’t bad necessarily, but I’ve grown to understand the importance of products such as serums, exfoliants, and of course SUNSCREEN. So now that I am learning more about my own skin, I thought I’d share what I am currently using on a daily and why. I assume this routine will be constantly changing with some things being altered and other steps being added, but I’m pretty happy with where I’ve started and am excited to share what is working for me at the moment.



Winter Lentil & Sweet Potato Soup


Ingredients (4-5 servings)

  • 1 small onion
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 peeled & diced sweet potato
  • 3 chopped carrots
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup lentils
  • Slice of lemon
  • Handful of chopped kale
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tbsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tbsp Trader Joe's Everday Seasoning (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, throw in your garlic, onion & a drizzle of olive oil on medium heat. Cook for 2-3 minutes while continually stirring.
  2. Season with garlic powder, black pepper, & curry powder (*Trader Joe's Everyday Seasoning optional)
  3. Add in carrots & sweet potatoes with some additional olive oil if needed. Stir & cover the pot for 5 minutes.
  4. Put your 4 cups of vegetable broth in and stir, covering again until it is boiling
  5. Once it is boiling, wash & add in your cup of lentils, stirring it in & covering once more. Cook for 15-20 minutes or until the lentils are soft.
  6. Throw in your desired amount of kale, squeeze the lemon slice juice in the pot, & stir. This is the perfect time to add a little bit more of the spices if you'd like. Cover the pot for 2 minutes to soften kale.
  7. Serve with a baguette or any side of choice.
